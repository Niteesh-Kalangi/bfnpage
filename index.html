Georgia's award-winning Black-Owned Farms

Honoring the Legacy of Black Farmers

Farming has been a vital part of Georgia's economy, culture, and overall identity for centuries. The Centennial Farms program, developed by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs in 1993, aims to distinguish family farms that have been in operation in Georgia for 100 years or more in order to honor their contributions to the preservation of Georgia’s rich agricultural history.

Despite the significant contributions of these Centennial award-winning farms, the stories of Black farmers who have received this prestigious award have been historically underrepresented. Black farmers have played an incredibly important role in shaping Georgia's agricultural landscape, despite facing challenges including discrimination, land loss, and economic hardships.

The goal of the Black Farmers’ Network is to amplify the stories of these historic, award-winning Black-owned farms and spread the stories of their rich histories and significant contributions.
